Get involved!

TampereJS is a community of web developers and a regular meetup revolving around JavaScript and web development in general. It is organized on first Thursday of every month, each time with different talks, sponsor and location. Also, there are drinks and snacks. So, if you are a web developer (or just web-curious) TampereJS is the place to be!

We are already over 400 strong and growing. Come join us for a drink or two!

Give a talk

Built something interesting? Learned something new? Found something awesome? Come and share the fun and know-how about it. Talks are the thing that the meetups live around so they are always welcome! Oh, and no pressure, we will contact you before listing your talk to an event, so you won't be thrown to the spotlight without warning. Talk should not be longer than 15-20 minutes, people come from work after all so keep it light.

Bonus: You get to do a shoutout to your company

TampereJS sponsor guidelines

We thank you for your humble contribution to the community! Here are few tips and guidelines to help you, as a sponsor, to get most out of TampereJS.

Before the event
  • Make sure you have space for at least 25 people
  • Send the address and the capacity of the location at least three(3) weeks before the event (event will be published two weeks in advance)
  • Submit the title and description of your JavaScript related talk at least three(3) weeks in advance (if you don't have one, organizers can help)
  • Tweet, Facebook, Snapchat like a MF (remember to tweet with "@TampereJS #TampereJS" so we can retweet you)
Just before/during the event
  • Tweet from the event (remember to tweet with "@TampereJS #TampereJS" so we can retweet you)
  • People start appearing 30 minutes before the event so be ready at that point (signs, snacks, drinks)
  • Have TampereJS signs leading from the street address all the way to where talks are held
  • Have someones phone number on the first sign in case someone gets lost or doors are locked
  • Have drinks and snacks available at the beginning so people can eat & drink while they wait
  • Have vegetarian and non-alcoholic options
  • Finnish are pretty quiet so have some music in the background while waiting for the talks to begin and after the talks
  • First talks usually start 15-25 minutes past the starting time, once everyone has arrived
  • You can have short company presentation at the beginning (max. 5 minutes) and you get the first talk
  • When the talks are over, there will be networking/mingling for hour/few hours so prepare for that
  • If you have a favorite bar nearby, you can recommend that as a "afterparty location" (usually bunch of people will go for few drinks after the meetup)
After the event
  • Post the presentation decks or link to the community in Facebook & Twitter
